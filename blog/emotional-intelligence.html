EQ

What is Emotional Intelligence.

Emotional Intelligence (EI) is the skill to understand and handle your own emotions while also recognizing and influencing the emotions of others. It helps you stay calm and motivated, communicate effectively, and build strong relationships. By being aware of your feelings and the feelings of those around you, you can navigate social situations better and make smarter decisions.


How is EQ different to IQ.

IQ (Intelligence Quotient) measures how well you think, solve problems, remember things, and use language. These tests are designed to compare people of various ages and backgrounds.
EQ (Emotional Intelligence) looks at how well you understand and manage emotions, including your own and othersâ€™. It helps you handle social situations better by focusing on feelings and relationships.


Why is EQ important.

Emotional Intelligence (EQ) is exceptionally important because it directly impacts how well you navigate social interactions, manage stress, and make decisions. High EQ enables you to handle difficult situations with empathy and understanding, leading to better relationships and teamwork. It helps you stay strong and adjust to changes, which is important for both personal and professional growth. While IQ shows your cognitive skills, EQ determines how well you use those skills in everyday situations, making it crucial for long-term success and well-being.
