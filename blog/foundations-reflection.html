Foundation Reflection

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the Human Skills learning?

I've become more aware of myself, which helps me understand my thoughts and emotions and how they affect what I do. I've also realized that my ego can sometimes get in the way, but now I’m more careful to manage it, especially when I interact with others or make decisions. I’ve also found that I’m more open to learning and growing, which makes me excited and motivates me to keep improving.


What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Values, empathy, and self-awareness are important in both learning and programming. Values guide me how to learn and code, helping me stay focused on what matters, like being ethical and responsible. Empathy allows us to understand others’ perspectives, which improves teamwork and helps to grow as a team. And Self-awareness is crucial because it allows me to recognize my strengths and limitations, driving personal improvement that positively impacts my coding journey.


What has surprised you the most about the Human Skills learning?

When I first started focusing on human skills, I didn’t think they would make much of a difference. However, after Sprint 2, I began to realize how they have transformed the way I approach coding and learning. Concepts like neuroplasticity and mindset growth have especially changed my perspective, helping me understand how adaptable my brain is and how this flexibility improves both my learning process and coding skills.


What were the most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning?

Rewiring old habits through the concept of neuroplasticity can be challenging because it involves changing the brain’s established pathways.


Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on Human Skills learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

Because technical skills alone aren't enough for success. Human skills like communication, teamwork, and self-awareness are crucial for working well with others, solving problems creatively, and adapting to new challenges. These skills help developers collaborate effectively, handle client interactions, and keep learning as the tech world evolves. By building both technical and human skills, the Bootcamp prepares students to be well-rounded and successful in their careers.


Does the time you spent studying Human Skills learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

As I mentioned earlier, after Sprint 2, my perspective changed. I realized that Human Skills are essential for growth in both personal and professional life. They help me not only become a better programmer but also improve how I communicate, collaborate, and adapt in different situations.
