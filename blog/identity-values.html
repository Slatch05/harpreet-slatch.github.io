Identity Values

Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

One of my long-term employees, who had been with the company for over three years and had earned my trust, was unfortunately caught engaging in misconduct on site. This was unexpected, as she had always demonstrated reliability and professionalism. I faced a difficult decision, as I valued her contributions and had a good working relationship with her. I immediately disclosed the situation to my superior to ensure transparency. After careful consideration, I decided to handle the matter internally without involving law enforcement. I believed that addressing the situation in a way that allowed her to exit the company respectfully, while maintaining her dignity, was a balanced approach. My goal was to resolve the issue without unnecessarily damaging her future career prospects, while also upholding company standards and integrity. Ultimately, I held a private meeting with her, explained the gravity of the situation, and we mutually agreed on her departure from the company. This allowed us to handle the situation professionally and respectfully, ensuring a smooth resolution without escalating tensions or creating a negative atmosphere. This version frames the situation professionally and emphasizes the balance between maintaining integrity and handling the matter with compassion.


Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

As a Sikh, my values and identity are shaped by core principles such as Seva (selfless service), equality, and honesty. The teachings of Sikhism, like the emphasis on hard work and standing up for justice, guide my personal and professional life. My strong family ties and involvement in the Sikh community reinforce values like humility, compassion, and service.


Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

My strengths will greatly benefit my studies as a software developer. My problem-solving skills will help me effectively address coding challenges and understand complex programming concepts by breaking them into smaller, manageable tasks. A strong work ethic will keep me disciplined and focused, ensuring I thoroughly grasp each topic before progressing. My quick learning ability allows me to swiftly adapt to new programming languages and technologies, keeping pace with the ever-evolving tech field.


Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

n evaluating my limitations in terms of learning and career development, I recognize a few potential challenges. My tendency to go down rabbit holes, exploring topics in depth that I find particularly interesting, can sometimes lead to spending excessive time on less critical areas, which might slow overall progress. Additionally, my perfectionism can cause delays as I might focus too much on refining details rather than advancing with my learning objectives. I also tend to become frustrated with slow progress, which can affect my motivation and persistence during more challenging phases of my journey. Moreover, balancing multiple learning goals or projects simultaneously can be difficult, potentially impacting my focus and efficiency. To address these issues, I plan to set clear goals, manage my time effectively, and seek regular feedback to ensure I stay on track and maintain a balanced approach to my learning and career development.


Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

As a store manager, I noticed that some team members weren’t working as hard as others. To fix this, I planned ahead and divided the tasks more fairly so everyone could contribute equally. This approach led to better results. Looking back, I see how important planning is. In the future, I’ll start each week by reviewing and scheduling tasks with proper time for each, to get better outcomes.
