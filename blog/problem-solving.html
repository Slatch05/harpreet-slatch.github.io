Problem Solving

Were blocked on a simple problem:

I encountered an issue with spacing in my code, which had me stuck for over 30 minutes before I finally reached out for help on Discord. I applied various problem-solving techniques, including step-by-step debugging, researching online, and breaking the code down into smaller sections. Ultimately, the issue was something very very simple. To better understand what was happening, I used console.log("Hello World") to check the flow of the program. This experience taught me that even the smallest details, like spacing, can prevent code from functioning properly.


Elegantly solved a problem.

I found the JS-Olympics challenges to be easier compared to others, particularly the task of removing odd numbers. While it wasn’t a difficult problem, I was able to solve it in the simplest, most efficient, and effective way I could think of. The approach I used made me feel accomplished because it not only worked well but also adhered to best practices. I focused on writing code that was clean and concise, using the least amount of time and resources while maintaining both clarity and functionality. One of the key factors I considered was readability—anyone looking at the code should be able to understand it easily without additional explanation. By ensuring that the solution was both elegant and straightforward, I was able to achieve a balance between performance and simplicity. Solving the problem this way gave me a sense of satisfaction, knowing that I didn’t overcomplicate it and that my solution was as effective as possible.
