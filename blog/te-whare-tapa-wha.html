Te Whare Tapa Whā

Te Whare Tapa Whā, developed by Sir Mason Durie in 1984, views health as a meeting house with four walls. These represent spiritual wellbeing (taha wairua), mental and emotional wellbeing (taha hinengaro), physical wellbeing (taha tinana), and family/social wellbeing (taha whānau), with the land (whenua) as the foundation. When all are balanced, we thrive, but if one is weakened, our wellbeing is affected.


Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing)

- I go to the gym five times a week for fitness.
- On weekend I aim to go hiking for atleast three hours.
- Drink 8 glasses of water per day.

Taha Whānau (Family Wellbeing)

- Eat dinner daily with family.
- Video call five times per week with parenets living oversea.
- Try to help in the house with cooking and cleaning.

Taha Hinengaro (Emotional and Mental Wellbeing)

- Practice mindfulness or meditation for 30 mins each day to reduce stress and improve focus.
- Practice tabla instrument to divert mind.

Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing)

- Prayer to connect inner self.
- Meditation and chatting name.
- Going to temple onces a week.

Whenua (Interconnections to the land and environment)

- Make sure to go outside every day with family.
- Walking barefoot on th grass for reduce inflammation, improve mood and reconnect to nature.
